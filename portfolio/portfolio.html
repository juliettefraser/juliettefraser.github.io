Writing Samples

"A Book Editor's Guide to Microsoft Word" (PDF)

For my Professional Writing and Technical Communications class, I crafted a 24-page Microsoft Word manual tailored to book editors. The final product was printed in color and spiral bound. This particular project taught me the foundations of writing technical documentation and offered profound insights into the field.

Grant Proposal for Second Chance Animal Services (PDF)

During my Introduction to Professional Writing class, I composed a 26-page faux grant proposal written on behalf of the nonprofit organization: Second Chance Animal Services. The document addresses the critical issues of limited access to veterinary care, pet food insecurity, animal overpopulation, and financial constraints. Pieces of this project were created with the aid of AI technology, as it was a vital theme of the course.

"Discovering Belonging Through Unconformity" (PDF)

Delving into the thematic intricacies of belonging and unconformity, my 10-page final paper for my American Studies class examines the two novels How Much of These Hills is Gold by C. Pam Zhang and Outlawed by Anna North. The paper also draws parallels from the essay on “Society” in Keywords for American Cultural Studies by Glenn Hendler.

"Seeking Forgiveness in a Conflicted World" (PDF)

My 8-page final research paper for my junior year English class explores the two novels, Cal by Bernard MacLaverty and Mother to Mother by Sindiwe Magona as they allegorize and problematize their characters into Christ figures. This in-depth analysis sheds light on the theme of conflicts both within literature and the real world.
